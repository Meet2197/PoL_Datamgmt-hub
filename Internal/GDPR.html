GDPR and Research Safeguards

A comprehensive legal study by the EDPB on appropriate safeguards under Article 89(1) of the GDPR for the processing of personal data for scientific research and other public interest purposes.

Key Findings

The study highlights the need for robust safeguards to balance data processing with fundamental rights. It focuses on the distinction between research and administrative purposes, emphasizing that research must be justified and adhere to ethical principles.

Scope and Application

Article 89(1) applies to processing for archiving, scientific/historical research, and statistical purposes. The study clarifies that these purposes must serve a public interest and are subject to specific legal bases, such as consent, public interest, or legitimate interest.

Safeguards in Practice

The legal study details a range of technical and organizational safeguards. These include data minimization, pseudonymisation, encryption, strict access controls, data retention limits, and regular data protection impact assessments (DPIAs).

Pseudonymisation and Anonymisation

These techniques are considered highly effective safeguards. The study distinguishes between the two, noting that pseudonymised data remains personal data, while anonymised data falls outside the GDPR's scope. Proper implementation is key.

Data Minimization

A core principle of GDPR, data minimization ensures that only data strictly necessary for the research purpose is processed. This includes using pseudonymised or anonymised data whenever possible to reduce the risk to individuals.

Conclusion and Recommendations

The study concludes that a combination of technical, organizational, and legal measures is necessary. It recommends a risk-based approach, where the level of safeguards corresponds to the nature and sensitivity of the data being processed for research.
