FAQs & Helpful Info for Researchers

What is proteomics data?

Proteomics is the large-scale study of proteins. Proteomics data, often generated by mass spectrometry, includes information about protein identification, quantification, and post-translational modifications.

Why should I submit my data to PRIDE?

Submitting to PRIDE ensures your data is stored in a permanent, curated, and publicly accessible archive. This increases the visibility and reproducibility of your research and helps you comply with journal and funder requirements.

Is there a cost to use the PRIDE Database?

The PRIDE Database is a free and open-access resource, meaning there is no cost to submit, store, or access data. This is part of the EMBL-EBI's commitment to open science.

How do I prepare my data for submission?

The PRIDE documentation provides detailed instructions on how to prepare your data. This often involves converting your raw mass spectrometry files into a standard format and providing rich metadata to describe your experiments.
