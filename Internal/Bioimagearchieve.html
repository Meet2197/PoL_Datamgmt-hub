BioImage Archive

The BioImage Archive is a public repository for biological imaging data, ranging from cellular microscopy to large-scale organismal imaging. It's an essential resource for researchers who need to share large, complex image datasets and make them findable, accessible, and reusable according to the FAIR principles.

  • Specialized Repository: Focuses specifically on biological imaging data, providing appropriate metadata standards and storage.
  • EMBL-EBI Supported: Part of a larger ecosystem of life science data resources, ensuring reliability and long-term curation.
  • FAIR Data: Promotes the findability and reusability of your imaging data, increasing the impact of your research.

FAQs & Helpful Info for Researchers

What types of image data are accepted?

The BioImage Archive accepts a broad spectrum of biological images, including light microscopy, electron microscopy, and medical imaging data. It is primarily for raw, unprocessed image data that supports a scientific publication.

Is it mandatory to use this archive for my publications?

Many journals and funders require the underlying data for a publication to be made publicly available in a recognized repository. The BioImage Archive is an excellent choice for fulfilling these requirements for imaging data.

How does this differ from general-purpose repositories?

A specialized archive like this provides specific tools and metadata standards for imaging data, making it easier for other researchers to find and re-use your work. It's a curated and more targeted resource than a general repository.

Do I need to be affiliated with EMBL-EBI to submit?

No, the BioImage Archive is a public resource open to all researchers. You simply need to create an account to begin the submission process.
