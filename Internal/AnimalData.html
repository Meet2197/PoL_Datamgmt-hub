Animal Research Data Management

Legal Framework

Data must comply with the German Animal Welfare Act (TierSchG) and EU Directive 2010/63/EU, covering all aspects of animal protection and experimentation.

Genetic Engineering Act

Research involving genetic modification of animals must also adhere to the strict requirements of the German Genetic Engineering Act (GenTG).

Experimentation Ordinance

The Animal Experimentation Ordinance (TierSchVersV) mandates specific data and documentation requirements for animal experiments and studies.

PyRAT Documentation

Use PyRAT to document all animal housing, breeding, and experimental procedures for complete tracking of samples.

The 3Rs Principle

The 3R principle (Replacement, Reduction, Refinement) must be evident in experimental design and documented in the DMP.

Detailed Metadata

Include detailed metadata on origin, strain, age, sex, and housing conditions to maximize data reusability.

Unique Identification

Animals must be uniquely identifiable with an ID number linked to their specific metadata.

Ethics Approval

Ethics committee approvals for animal experiments must be digitally linked to all resulting data.

Statistical Reporting

Annual statistical records must be kept on species, origin, number, purpose, and severity of animal use.
